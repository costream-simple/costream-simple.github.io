CoStream: Compositional Multi-Stream Framework for Multi-Modal Manipulation

Paper

CoStream demonstrates robust contact-rich manipulation with real-time recovery from external perturbations during CPU, GPU, and RAM component assembly.

Abstract

Long-horizon contact-rich manipulation tasks demand that robots bridge the gap between slow, deliberate planning and high-frequency, reactive control. Classical pipelines struggle with the brittle modularity of hand-engineered components, whereas monolithic end-to-end policies lack the flexibility to recombine learned skills for new scenarios or compositional generalization.

We propose CoStream, a multi-stream architecture that combines foundation models (LLMs, VLMs, world models) with complementary modalities (vision, language, force) to achieve compositional generalization. These streams function as autonomous, parallel processes that generate different forms of complementary, non-blocking information: the semantic stream applies abstract reasoning for task decomposition and constraint specification, the visual imagination stream utilizes world models to generate trajectories, and the reactive stream generates high-frequency compliant corrective behaviors during physical contact.

Outputs are integrated into a final action through the multiplexing of object-centric transformations. This compositional design enables reuse of modules across different abstraction levels while maintaining closed-loop stability and asynchronous reactivity even when high-level semantic reasoning or visual imagination introduce large computational latency.

We validate CoStream on long-horizon contact-rich assembly tasks including RAM, CPU, and GPU motherboard component insertion and drill insertion. Our approach substantially outperforms prior methods, achieving a 96.7% mean success rate with sub-millimeter (0.5 mm) precision and demonstrates robustness through recovery against external human perturbations during execution.

Method Overview

CoStream decomposes manipulation into three parallel streams: Semantic (LLMs/VLMs for task decomposition), Visual (world models for trajectory generation), and Reactive (tactile/force feedback for compliant control). Streams synchronize through object-centric transformation composition.

Related Work

CoStream builds on recent advances in foundation models for robotics and contact-rich manipulation.

Foundation Models in Robotics: Recent work has explored using large language models and vision-language models for robot control, including VoxPoser for compositional manipulation and RT-1 for generalist robotics policies.

Contact-Rich Manipulation: Tactile sensing and force control have been explored for precision manipulation tasks, with methods leveraging GelSight sensors and compliant control strategies.

Compositional Approaches: Task and Motion Planning (TAMP) and modular policy architectures provide structured approaches to decompose complex manipulation tasks, though they often require extensive manual engineering.

World Models for Robotics: Video generation models have recently been applied to robot planning, enabling visual imagination of future trajectories for motion planning and control.
