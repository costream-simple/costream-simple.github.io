Abstract

Long-horizon, contact-rich complex manipulation tasks — such as seating a GPU into a PCIe slot — demand both millimeter precision and out-of-the-box generalization to new tasks. Classical pipelines use brittle, task-specific interfaces to achieve high-precision control but require costly redesigns to adapt to new tasks, whereas monolithic end-to-end policies provide better generalization but lack high precision on complex, out-of-distribution tasks unless retrained with new data. Both paradigms share an implicit assumption: once a manipulation capability is acquired, it must be deployed as a rigid pipeline or monolithic whole, rather than being freely decomposed and recomposed.

In this paper, we show that complex manipulation capabilities can emerge naturally from the composition of simple, independent behaviors. We propose CoStream, a framework orchestrating foundation models and diverse sensing modalities into multiple composable core behaviors: a semantic behavior extracting spatial constraints via foundation models; a predictive behavior forecasting trajectories by tracking keypoints in imagined videos; and a reactive behavior providing high-frequency tactile and force corrections.

On a shared SE(3) interface, these outputs compose by right-multiplication into a single pose command at each control step, executed by a compliant controller. We demonstrate CoStream on 8 real-world tasks spanning everyday manipulation and precision assembly, with the strongest gains in contact-rich assembly and object transfer, and show robust recovery from manual perturbations during execution.

Method Overview

CoStream decomposes manipulation into three parallel behaviors: Semantic (LLMs/VLMs extract object-centric spatial constraints), Predictive (a video world model forecasts a nominal trajectory via keypoint tracking), and Reactive (tactile/force feedback for high-frequency compliant corrections). On a shared SE(3) interface, the behaviors compose by right-multiplication into a single pose command at every control step.

Figure 2: CoStream Architecture. A scene parser converts language goals and observations into a Scene Summary, which a contact-aware policy compiler expands into typed stages. Within each stage, three behaviors run in parallel at their natural rates: the Semantic behavior produces a task-frame anchor, the Predictive behavior generates a nominal motion prior from imagined video, and the Reactive behavior emits a tactile residual. An action composer fuses them by right-multiplication into one pose command per control step, executed by a compliant controller.

Predictive Behavior

The predictive behavior uses a video world model to "imagine" candidate execution trajectories conditioned on the current image and step instruction. A VLM critic selects the rollout that best satisfies the semantic and safety constraints, and a 3D keypoint tracker lifts it into an object-centric nominal motion prior.

Task Performance

We evaluate CoStream on 8 real-world tasks (15 trials each) across two groups: high-precision contact-rich assembly and everyday manipulation. The same set of behaviors transfers across all tasks without per-task retraining.

Failure Cases

While CoStream achieves a 96.7% mean success rate, we analyze the remaining failure cases to understand current limitations. Failures arise from two main sources: task-level perturbations that exceed the reactive stream's correction capacity, and semantic reasoning errors during multi-stage planning.

GPU Perturbation Failure

A large-magnitude perturbation displaces the GPU beyond the reactive behavior's recovery range, resulting in a failed insertion attempt.

RAM LLM Stage Failure

An incorrect stage transition predicted by the LLM semantic behavior causes the robot to proceed prematurely, resulting in a misaligned RAM insertion.

Long-Horizon Motherboard Assembly

CoStream successfully completes the full motherboard assembly sequence, transitioning seamlessly between CPU, GPU, and RAM insertion tasks. This demonstrates compositional generalization across multiple contact-rich manipulation subtasks without task-specific retraining.

Related Work

CoStream builds on recent advances in foundation models for robotics and contact-rich manipulation.

Foundation Models in Robotics: Recent work has explored using large language models and vision-language models for robot control, including VoxPoser for compositional manipulation and RT-1 for generalist robotics policies.

Contact-Rich Manipulation: Tactile sensing and force control have been explored for precision manipulation tasks, with methods leveraging GelSight sensors and compliant control strategies.

Compositional Modeling: Inspired by behavior-based robotics, where complex behavior emerges from composing simple sensory behaviors, CoStream composes semantic, predictive, and reactive behaviors in parallel over a shared SE(3) interface — avoiding both the hand-specified interfaces of Task and Motion Planning (TAMP) and the retraining requirements of monolithic end-to-end policies such as π0.5.

World Models for Robotics: Video generation models have recently been applied to robot planning, enabling visual imagination of future trajectories for motion planning and control.
